Insights &
Realizations

A reflection on the knowledge, challenges, and growth experienced throughout IT2301 Web Systems.

Key Learnings

Taking IT2301 Web Systems has been one of the most rewarding and eye-opening experiences in my academic journey as a BSIT student. Before this course, I had a basic understanding of what websites were and how they generally functioned, but I lacked the technical knowledge to actually build one from the ground up. Throughout this semester, I gained a comprehensive understanding of the full web development stack — from structuring content with HTML, styling interfaces with CSS, adding interactivity through JavaScript, to implementing server-side logic using PHP and storing data in MySQL databases.

One of the most significant realizations I had was understanding the relationship between front-end and back-end development. Learning how a form on a webpage can send data to a PHP script that processes and stores it in a database gave me a clear picture of how modern web applications actually work. This end-to-end understanding is something I will carry with me into my future career.

Challenges Encountered

The course was not without its difficulties. One of the biggest challenges I faced was debugging PHP and MySQL integration. Early on, I struggled with connecting to the database, handling errors gracefully, and ensuring that data was being retrieved and displayed correctly. There were moments of frustration when my queries returned unexpected results or when a missing semicolon caused the entire script to fail.

Another challenge was making my web pages fully responsive. Designing a layout that looks clean on both a desktop and a mobile screen required a deeper understanding of CSS, particularly Flexbox, Grid, and media queries. I initially underestimated how much effort goes into responsive design, and working through these layouts taught me patience and a systematic approach to problem-solving.

Strategies Used to Address Challenges

To overcome the technical challenges I encountered, I relied on a combination of self-study, online resources, and peer collaboration. Whenever I was stuck on a bug, I developed a habit of reading error messages carefully and using browser developer tools to inspect elements and trace issues. I also made use of documentation from MDN Web Docs and W3Schools, which provided clear explanations and practical examples that complemented what I learned in class.

For responsive design, I adopted a mobile-first approach — building for smaller screens first and then scaling up — which made the process more manageable. Breaking down complex tasks into smaller, testable steps also helped me stay focused and measure my progress more effectively.

Overall Insights and Realizations

Looking back at everything I have built this semester, I am genuinely proud of how far I have come. Web development is a field that requires both creativity and analytical thinking, and this course has helped me grow in both areas. I have learned that building something that works is just the beginning — the real craft lies in making it clean, efficient, accessible, and maintainable.

This course has also reinforced my interest in pursuing a career in web development. Every project, even the ones that frustrated me the most, contributed something valuable to my understanding and skills. I leave this course not just with a portfolio of outputs, but with a stronger mindset, a sharper eye for detail, and a genuine excitement for what lies ahead in my development journey.
